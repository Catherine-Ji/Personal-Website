Welcome to Seattle

Seattle, a city on Puget Sound in the Pacific Northwest, is surrounded by water, mountains and evergreen forests, and contains thousands of acres of parkland.
Washington State’s largest city, it’s home to a large tech industry, with Microsoft, Amazon, and F5 headquartered in its metropolitan area.

How to get here?

  • Take a plane to SeaTac International Airport! We have an excellent public transit system!
  • Given the busy nature of the area, travelling to and from Seattle is typically done by bus.

Tourist Attractions

  • Space Needle: The Space Needle is a common attraction in the Seattle city that most people think of when they picture the city. It is a tall, 605 foot structure located on Broad Street. There are 6 floors that make up the Space Needle. With a 360 degree view space on the top floor that looks over the city, this attraction is definitely one to experience.
  • Pike Place Market: Pike Place Market is a huge attraction in Seattle. With dozens of stores and shops and set ups, it is a very well known and popular market to explore. With a large variety of products to choose from including fish, flowers, fruits, and comic books, this is definitely a location that you could spend a lot of time exploring.
  • Gum Wall:The Gum Wall is a fun and interesting place in Seattle. Located under Pike Place Market, this alleyway is covered in chewed up gum pieces in seemingly every inch of the place. Makes for a fun place to check out. Just bring a piece of gum, chew it up, and find an empty spot on the wall to place it. Don’t forget to pull out your camera to document this fun moment!
